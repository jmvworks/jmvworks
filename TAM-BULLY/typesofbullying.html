WHAT IS BULLYING?

Bullying is a form of aggressive behavior in which a person intentionally and repeatedly causes discomfort to another person.
It includes intentional use of words or actions against someone or a group that causes risk to their well-being.

Types of Bullying

It includes hitting, kicking,
shoving, spitting, beating up,
stealing or damaging property.
It includes name-calling, mocking,
hurtful teasing, insults, slurs,
humiliating or threatening someone,
racist comments, or sexual
harassment.
It includes spreading rumors,
damaging reputations and
friendships, or setting others up
to look foolish.
It includes sharing personal or
private information online,
sending, posting, or sharing
negative and harmful
context about someone else.
It includes treating people badly
because of their racial or
ethnic background, saying bad
things about a cultural
background, calling someone
racist names or telling racist jokes.
It includes treating people badly
because of their religious
backgrounds or beliefs, making
negative comments about a religious
background, calling someone names
or telling jokes based on his or her
religious beliefs.
It includes making someone
feel uncomfortable because of
their gender, making sexual
comments, sexual harassment,
spreading sexual rumors, or making
cruel jokes about someone's
sexual behavior and identity.
It includes treating them badly
because of their disability, making
jokes about their condition, or
making someone feel uncomfortable.

How to Prevent Bullying?

Educate the Youth about Bullying and Empathy: Educate them about what bullying is and conduct campaigns or activities that highlight the importance of empathy.
Raise awareness about its effects: Spread knowledge about the mental and physical effects of bullying to everyone.
Report to a trusted adult: Talk to a professional, may it be a guidance counselor, parents, or teacher, about the incident.
Foster respect: Promote respect to individuals. Educate the youth on how to understand and appreciate one another.
